## 1. Executive summary

By **draining the water at the village collection yard first** and only then dispatching the nuts to Kangeyam, the project captures ~0.3 litre of free raw material per nut with **no loss to the copra business**.
- **Climate fit — the monsoon that blocks drying does NOT block vinegar.** Fermentation happens in **closed, temperature-controlled tanks indoors**, so the ~6-month rainy season that forces the village to outsource drying is irrelevant to this unit. The plant runs year-round in the same climate that rules out sun-dependent products.
- **Constitution:** sole proprietorship — **Arun T., proprietor**.
- **One unit, phased in place (not split):** start at **~2,000 nuts/day (~600 L water/day)** and **ramp to ~4,000 then ~6,000 nuts/day** by adding modular fermentation/acetification capacity within the **same registered premises**.
- **Purpose-built hall:** a **~8,000 sq ft food-grade hall** is built **once, up front, for the ~6,000 nuts/day endgame** (hygienic epoxy floor, drained wet-process zone, separate clean bottling zone, warehouse, QC lab). Sizing the civil + utilities + ETP for the endgame puts fixed capital just past the point that **captures the full ₹40 lakh Kerala ESS subsidy** (the scheme maximum) without overspending.
- **Pollution category — designed for the lenient regime with Zero-Liquid-Discharge (ZLD).** Effluent is dilute, biodegradable wash/CIP water; it is treated in a compact ETP and **reused on the greenbelt/plantation — nothing is discharged to land or water**. Solid residues (filter cake, spent yeast/mother) are **composted / diverted to the promoter's biochar unit**. A **written KSPCB categorisation confirmation** is sought (Section 7).
- **Product:** **coconut cider vinegar** in two lines — (a) **raw, unfiltered "with the mother"** (premium, apple-cider-vinegar-style health positioning) and (b) **filtered food-grade vinegar** in bulk for the pickle / food-processing / HoReCa trade (the volume anchor).
- **Regulatory backbone:** **FSSAI "brewed vinegar"** standard — minimum **3.75% acetic acid** (target 5%), no synthetic-acid fortification (Section 6, 10).
- **Project cost (Phase-1):** ~**₹1.01 crore** (~₹0.93 cr fixed capital + ₹8 L working-capital margin); land is the promoter's own (in-kind, excluded).
- **Financing & maximised subsidy stack:** promoter equity + **AIF term loan (3% interest subvention, 7 yrs)** + **CGTMSE** collateral-free cover + **Kerala ESS capital subsidy (~₹40 L — the scheme maximum)** + **PMFME/ODOP branding & marketing grant on the separate marketing head** (Section 12).
- **Cluster synergy:** shares land, utilities, QC and management with the promoter's **biochar / bio-fertilizer unit**; coconut **husk and shell** feed the biochar unit and vinegar **process residues** feed its compost/enrichment line (Section 16).

## 3. Project concept & rationale

Every mature coconut sent for copra carries ~0.3 litre of coconut water. In the present flow — **de-husk → ship whole nuts to Kangeyam → split & dry** — that water is **drained and wasted at Kangeyam**. Recovering it at source and fermenting it into vinegar:

1. **Monetises a zero-cost waste stream** without touching the existing copra income (the kernel still goes to Kangeyam exactly as before).
2. Produces a **shelf-stable, high-margin food product** (vinegar keeps for years — no cold chain), unlike the perishable water itself.
3. **Fits the local climate:** enclosed-tank fermentation is unaffected by the ~6-month monsoon that makes local sun-drying impossible.
4. **Qualifies for strong central + state subsidy** as an agro-based micro/small food-processing MSME (Section 12).
5. Is structured for the **lenient pollution category with ZLD**, so the unit can be established and grown in place under a light-touch consent regime (Section 7).

### 3.1 The "drain-first" logistics change (answering the core question)
Instead of shipping whole nuts, the yard will **drain the water and then dispatch the nuts to Kangeyam.** Two draining methods, with a clear recommendation:

| Method | How | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|
| Full split | Halve the nut, drain, ship halves | Maximum water recovery, but the **exposed kernel is vulnerable to mould/rancidity in humid transit** — a real copra-quality (and aflatoxin) risk during the monsoon |
| **Bored-eye drain (recommended)** | Pierce the soft "eye", drain the water, ship the near-intact nut | Recovers the water while the **shell keeps protecting the kernel** to Kangeyam — protects copra grade |

**Recommendation: bore-and-drain, keeping the shell intact**, so copra quality is preserved. Advantages captured: (a) free vinegar raw material; (b) **lower transport weight to Kangeyam** (~0.3 kg/nut of water removed); (c) less uncontrolled in-transit fermentation/leakage; (d) the copra process is otherwise unchanged. Confirm the commercial arrangement with the Kangeyam driers (whether they pay per nut or per kg — draining changes the weight).

2. **Branded ACV-style "with the mother" (primary margin).** Ride the wellness trend; sell through organic stores, pharmacies, and **e-commerce (Amazon/Flipkart)**. Requires branding + FSSAI-compliant labelling.

## 5. Raw-material plan (coconut water)

| Source | Basis |
|---|---|
| **Own ~10-acre coconut holding** | Captive / free — nuts routed through the yard for de-husking + water draining before Kangeyam dispatch |
| **Village cluster nuts aggregated for Kangeyam** | The yard already collects husked nuts for dispatch; water is drained here at near-zero cost under a supply understanding with the growers |
| **Jaggery / muscovado sugar** (process input, not waste) | Open-market — needed to raise the fermentable sugar level (see below) |

**Perishability — the binding operational rule.** Coconut water spoils within hours. It must be **filtered and pasteurised the same day it is drained**. Throughput is therefore matched to the daily nut flow through the yard, not stockpiled.

**Composition & why sugar must be added.** Mature (copra-grade) coconut water is thin — about **330 mL/nut at ~4.85 °Brix (~2.5 g sugar/100 mL)** — too low to ferment directly to legal-strength vinegar. It is **adjusted with jaggery/muscovado to ~15 °Brix** before fermentation, to reach ~7–8% alcohol and then ≥5% acetic acid. This is standard practice; note the FSSAI rule that the acidity must come from **fermentation, not from added synthetic acetic acid** (Section 6).

Execute a **written raw-material understanding** with the growers/aggregators routing nuts to Kangeyam, so water recovery is secured at source.

## 6. Technical design, process & capacity (single unit, phased in place)

### 6.1 Ramp path (one unit, capacity added in place — not split)

| Phase | Coconut water intake | Nuts/day equiv. | Vinegar output | Annual output (300 days) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase-1 (start)** | ~600 L/day | ~2,000 | ~600 L/day | ~1.8 lakh L/yr |
| **Phase-2** | ~1,200 L/day | ~4,000 | ~1,200 L/day | ~3.6 lakh L/yr |
| **Phase-3 (endgame)** | ~1,800 L/day | ~6,000 | ~1,800 L/day | ~5.4 lakh L/yr |

Capacity is grown by **adding modular fermenters/acetators inside the same hall** — the unit is never split into separate registrations. The **hall, utilities, boiler, ETP and bottling line are built once for the endgame**, so ramping needs only extra tankage.

### 6.2 Process (two-stage fermentation)

```
Drained coconut water (same day)
  -> Filter / screen -> Pasteurise 60-65 C, 10-15 min -> cool
  -> Adjust to ~15 Brix with jaggery/muscovado; add yeast nutrient
  -> STAGE 1  Alcoholic fermentation (Saccharomyces cerevisiae),
              anaerobic, ~7-14 days  ->  "coconut cider" ~7-8% ABV
  -> Rack / decant off yeast; pasteurise; cool
  -> STAGE 2  Acetous fermentation (Acetobacter aceti + mother of vinegar),
              aerobic; slow surface method ~3-4 wk OR submerged acetator ~1-2 wk
              ->  vinegar, acidity >= 5%
  -> Age / clarify -> filter -> standardise acidity
  -> LINE A (raw): keep unfiltered "with the mother", cold-fill (premium)
     LINE B (food-grade): pasteurise, filter bright, bulk-fill
  -> Bottle / label / batch-code -> QC release -> warehouse
```

### 6.3 Product quality & FSSAI compliance
To be sold legally as vinegar in India (FSSAI "brewed vinegar" standard), each batch must meet:
- **Acidity ≥ 3.75% (w/v) as acetic acid** (the plan targets ~5% for a robust product),
- **Total solids ≥ 1.5%**, **total ash ≥ 0.18%**,
- **No mineral acids**, and it **must not be fortified with synthetic acetic acid** — the acid must come from fermentation.

**QC lab** performs titratable-acidity titration, pH, residual-alcohol and basic microbiological checks on every batch, with batch coding for traceability.

### 6.4 Climate & utility note
Fermentation is enclosed and mildly temperature-controlled; the monsoon is a non-issue. Main utilities: potable/softened water, a small steam boiler for pasteurisation/CIP, 3-phase power, and a compact cold room for the raw "with-mother" line.

## 7. Pollution-control category & consent strategy

**Objective: establish the unit in the lenient pollution category — legitimately, on the merits — with Zero-Liquid-Discharge.**

### 7.1 Pollution profile (genuinely low)
| Stream | Nature | Management |
|---|---|---|
| **Liquid effluent** | Dilute, biodegradable **wash / CIP / floor-wash water**; small volume | Compact **ETP** (screening → equalisation → anaerobic + aerobic → clarifier) → **treated water reused on greenbelt/plantation. ZLD — no discharge to land or water.** |
| **Solid residue** | Filter cake, spent yeast, surplus "mother" | **Composted / diverted to the biochar–bio-fertilizer unit** (Section 16) |
| **Air** | Minor steam / faint fermentation odour; no combustion process gases beyond a small boiler | Enclosed process; boiler stack to norms; greenbelt buffer |
| **Hazard** | None significant — no solvents, no synthetic acids, no alcohol bottled for sale | — |

### 7.2 Categorisation strategy
Food fermentation can be viewed two ways by the Board, so this is handled explicitly rather than assumed:
- The **RED "fermentation industry"** entry in the CPCB/KSPCB list targets **distilleries, breweries and malt units producing potable/industrial alcohol.** This unit is **not** that: **no alcohol is bottled or sold** (ethanol is only a fleeting in-process intermediate), scale is small, and effluent is minor and organic.
- General **food & food-processing** activity of this size, with **ZLD and a working ETP**, fits the **Green/Orange (lenient) regime.**
- **Action:** design to the lenient category on the merits (ZLD, ETP, no discharge) and **obtain a written KSPCB categorisation confirmation** *before* sanction — mirroring the approach used for the promoter's biochar unit. Sanction should follow that written confirmation.

### 7.3 Consent path
- **CTE (Consent to Establish) then CTO (Consent to Operate)** from KSPCB under the Water Act 1974 and Air Act 1981; amend the CTO upward on each ramp.
- **No Environmental Clearance** (not an EIA-2006 scheduled activity at this scale).
- Operating without CTE/CTO is an offence under the EPA 1986 — consents are obtained before commissioning.

### 12.1 How the subsidy is maximised
The plan is engineered so that **one large capital subsidy is captured at its ceiling, and only non-overlapping supports are stacked on top** (two capital subsidies cannot fund the same asset — "double-dipping" is disallowed and voids both).

- **Anchor = Kerala ESS capital subsidy — target ~₹40 L (the scheme maximum).** At the **~45% band** (25% base for young/women/SC-ST/NRK entrepreneur + 10% agro-based priority sector + 10% new-technology/first-generation), the ₹40 L cap is reached at ~₹89 L eligible fixed capital. The project's **~₹93 L fixed capital just clears that point, so the full ₹40 L is captured without overspending.** *(Palakkad does not attract the extra district bonus.)* ESS is received as **reimbursement after commissioning** and applied to **prepay the term loan**.
- **+ AIF (central) — 3% interest subvention (7 yrs) + CGTMSE guarantee.** This is an *interest/credit* benefit on a **different basis**, so it **stacks legitimately with ESS**. Effective term-loan rate ≈ 9% − 3% = **~6%**; the loan is **collateral-free** (land not mortgaged).
- **+ PMFME / ODOP branding & marketing grant — on the separate marketing head.** PMFME's branding-and-marketing support (up to 50% of eligible marketing spend, and ODOP linkage for coconut products) funds a **different expense (marketing, not plant)**, so it can be added without disturbing ESS on the capital.

### 12.2 Why ESS is the right anchor (decision logic)
| Scheme | Rate | Ceiling | Subsidy on ~₹93 L project | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Kerala ESS** | up to ~45% | **₹40 L** | **~₹40 L** | **Anchor — largest for this size** |
| CDB Technology Mission on Coconut (product diversification) | ~25% | ₹50 L | ~₹23 L | Beats ESS only if project > ~₹1.6 cr |
| PMFME (micro food processing) capital subsidy | 35% | ₹10 L | ~₹10 L | Smaller cap; use its *marketing* head instead |
| PMKSY unit scheme | 35% (50% difficult areas) | ₹5 cr | (large-unit scheme) | Consider only if scaling to a much larger plant |

- **For this ~₹1 crore plant, Kerala ESS (₹40 L) is the maximum** capital subsidy. CDB or PMKSY would only win at much larger project sizes; if the promoter later scales past ~₹1.6 cr, re-evaluate CDB/PMKSY as the anchor.
- **Do NOT use PMEGP** as a capital subsidy — availing it disqualifies the unit from other government subsidies and would **void the far larger ESS + AIF benefit**.
- **Confirm ESS + AIF convergence in writing** with DIC Palakkad and the lending bank before sanction.

### 12.3 Net effect
ESS ₹40 L prepays the loan, cutting effective debt to ~₹26 L; AIF trims interest to ~6%; CGTMSE removes the collateral requirement; PMFME/ODOP part-funds branding. **Net capital at risk (Phase-1) ≈ ₹61 L** after ESS.
