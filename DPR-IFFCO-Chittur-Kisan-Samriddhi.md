## 1. Executive summary

The three target panchayats form the **driest, most water-stressed corner of Kerala** — the rain-shadow belt along the Palakkad Gap on the Tamil Nadu border — yet they have received **little dedicated IFFCO development investment**, despite being an intensely agrarian region growing paddy, groundnut, coconut, sugarcane and vegetables with a large small-holder and Tamil-speaking farming community.

This DPR proposes a **₹10 crore, three-year catch-up programme**, delivered through the **Chittur Agriculture Marketing Co-operative Society**, structured around four outcomes:

1. **Secure water** — recharge, harvesting structures and micro-irrigation (the binding constraint; 35% of outlay).
2. **Cut input cost and lift yields** — soil testing, balanced nutrition and Nano Urea / Nano DAP.
3. **Improve realised prices** — cooperative storage, grading, primary processing and market linkage to IFFCO Bazar / iMandi.
4. **Build a self-sustaining, farmer-owned service institution** — an agri-service hub, custom hiring centre, drone service and FPO owned and operated by the cooperative.

The programme is designed so that the **revenue-generating assets pay for their own operation** after the grant, and so that the ₹10 crore is **leveraged further** through convergence with PMKSY, RKVY, watershed, FPO and state schemes plus NCDC/bank credit.

## 2. The equity case — why this region, why now

- **Chronic neglect.** IFFCO's flagship rural-development, model-village and CORDET-type investments have historically concentrated near its plant and operational hubs in other states, largely **bypassing this border belt.**
- **Highest need, lowest support.** The region faces **Kerala's harshest rainfall deficit and groundwater stress**, heavy borewell dependence, and rising input costs — precisely the problems IFFCO's product-and-fund basket is built to solve — yet it has not been a beneficiary.
- **A ready, trusted delivery vehicle already exists** in the Chittur Agriculture Marketing Co-operative Society, so a grant converts directly into on-ground delivery, not new overheads.
- **Cooperative-to-cooperative equity.** As a farmer-owned cooperative, IFFCO investing in an under-served cooperative-farming community inside its own TN/Kerala/Puducherry zone is squarely within its mandate.

**In short: a region that has long powered Palakkad's agriculture has been left off IFFCO's development map. This ₹10 crore corrects that.**

## 3. Situation analysis

### 3.1 Agro-climatic context
The three panchayats sit in the Palakkad Gap rain-shadow, receiving markedly **lower and more erratic rainfall** than the rest of Kerala. Agriculture depends heavily on **borewells and lift irrigation**, and groundwater tables are under stress from over-extraction. Summer water scarcity is acute and recurrent.

### 3.2 Cropping and community
- **Principal crops:** paddy, groundnut, coconut, sugarcane, and a strong **vegetable economy**; mango and pulses in pockets.
- **Community:** a large population of small, marginal and tenant farmers, significant women's participation (Kudumbashree), and a sizeable Tamil-speaking farming population reflecting the border location.

### 3.3 Core problems
1. **Water insecurity** and falling water tables; over-dependence on borewells.
2. **High input costs** and unbalanced fertilisation degrading soil health.
3. **Post-harvest losses** and weak price bargaining power (distress sales at harvest).
4. **Limited access** to modern services — soil testing, machinery, drones, advisory, insurance.
5. **An under-utilised marketing cooperative** operating well below its potential.

### 3.4 Opportunity
IFFCO's product and service basket, plus its social funds, map almost exactly onto these gaps, and a locally trusted cooperative exists to deliver them. This alignment makes the region an ideal, high-impact, replicable investment.

The programme advances IFFCO's core objectives: **strengthening the cooperative movement**, promoting **balanced and sustainable fertilisation (including Nano Urea and Nano DAP)**, improving **farmer incomes and welfare**, and extending **rural development and natural-resource management**. It is therefore a strong fit for IFFCO Foundation / cooperative-development funding, the GVT integrated-development model, and IFFCO-Tokio CSR.

## 5. Goal and objectives

**Goal:** Sharply raise the water security, farm income and resilience of ~[10,000–12,000] farming families across the three panchayats through a cooperative-led, IFFCO-funded programme.

**Objectives**
1. Bring **[1,500] acres** under assured micro-irrigation and build **[540] water recharge/harvesting structures**.
2. Reduce **input cost per acre by [20]%** and improve soil health across **[10,000] farms** via balanced/nano nutrition.
3. Establish a fully equipped **Cooperative Agri-Service Hub** (inputs, soil testing, machinery, drones, advisory, insurance, marketing).
4. Create **storage, grading and primary value-addition** capacity and link produce to IFFCO Bazar / iMandi.
5. Extend **insurance/welfare cover** to **[8,000] families** and scholarships to **[500] students**.
6. Make the cooperative's new service lines **financially self-sustaining by Year 3.**

## 6. Programme components, specifications and physical targets

### Component 1 — Water security & natural-resource management *(anchor, ₹350 lakh)*
- **Micro-irrigation (drip/sprinkler)** on ~[1,500] acres, prioritising vegetables, groundnut and coconut, with IFFCO as top-up over PMKSY subsidy.
- **Recharge structures** — ~[300] borewell/open-well recharge pits and rooftop/field recharge.
- **Farm ponds** — ~[200] lined/unlined ponds for supplemental and lifesaving irrigation.
- **Check dams / water-body revival** — ~[40] structures and desilting/restoration of local ponds and canals.
- Micro-watershed treatment on identified catchments across the three panchayats; user/beneficiary groups formed for maintenance.

### Component 2 — Soil health & nano/balanced nutrition *(₹50 lakh)*
- **Mini soil-testing facility** at the hub plus ~[10,000] soil samples and **soil-health cards** with crop-specific nutrient plans.
- ~[300] **Nano Urea / Nano DAP demonstration plots** on paddy, groundnut, vegetables and coconut, with measured yield and cost data.
- Promotion of **bio-fertilisers, water-soluble and specialty products**, and integrated nutrient management (INM).

### Component 3 — Cooperative Agri-Service Hub *(₹180 lakh)*
- **Hub building** (~[3,000] sq ft): input depot, soil lab, advisory desk, training hall, office and secure storage.
- **IFFCO input depot** fit-out and opening stock — fertilisers, nano products, bio-inputs, seeds, PP chemicals at cooperative rates.
- **Custom Hiring Centre (CHC)** — tractors, power tillers, sprayers, seed drills, groundnut decorticator, paddy thresher and dryers for member rental.
- **Kisan Drone service** — ~[3] agri-drones with batteries/charging and trained local pilots, for nano-fertiliser and PP spraying.
- **Digital advisory** — enrolment on the IFFCO Kisan app plus Malayalam/Tamil voice/SMS advisory with weather and mandi-price alerts.

### Component 4 — Market linkage & value addition *(₹210 lakh)*
- **Godown/storage** (~[2,000] MT) to cut distress sales and post-harvest loss.
- **Grading, cleaning and weighing** unit for quality-based pricing.
- **Primary processing** — groundnut decortication and oil expeller, mini rice unit, cleaning and packaging.
- **FPO** federating farmers across the three panchayats; **branding** and onboarding onto **IFFCO Bazar / iMandi** with buyer tie-ups.

### Component 5 — Welfare & risk cover *(₹60 lakh)*
- **IFFCO-Tokio** crop, cattle, health and accident cover for ~[8,000] families, with premium support and enrolment camps.
- **Kisan Sewa Trust** scholarships for ~[500] farmers' children and a distress-relief fund.

### Component 6 — Capacity building *(₹60 lakh)*
- **Farmer training** for ~[5,000] farmers (CORDET-linked) on water-efficient and balanced-nutrition practices, FPO management and digital tools.
- **Drone-pilot and agri-entrepreneur training** for ~[30] local youth.
- Annual **Kisan Mela** (three editions) with KVK Palakkad / KAU.
- **IEC / awareness** in Malayalam and Tamil, and advisory-app onboarding.

### Component 7 — Programme management, M&E and contingency *(₹90 lakh)*
- **Project Management Unit** (project manager, two field coordinators, MIS/accounts) for three years.
- **Baseline and endline surveys** and third-party evaluation.
- **Audit, documentation and digital dashboard.**
- **Contingency** for cost variation.

## 9. Implementation model and institutional arrangement

- The **Chittur Agriculture Marketing Co-operative Society** is the single implementing platform and **owns the created assets** (hub, machinery, drones, storage, processing). IFFCO provides the grant, products, technology and training.
- A **Project Management Committee (PMC)** governs delivery, with representation from IFFCO's Kerala State Marketing Office, the three panchayats, the Chittur Municipality and the Society's board.
- **KVK Palakkad / Kerala Agricultural University** is the technical partner for agronomy, demonstrations and training.
- A dedicated **Project Management Unit (PMU)** handles day-to-day execution, MIS and accounts.

## 13. Monitoring, evaluation and KPIs

- **Baseline → endline** survey of income, input cost, yield, water table and insurance coverage.
- **Tracked KPIs:** acres under micro-irrigation; recharge structures built; % input-cost reduction; nano-adoption rate; drone-service acres; produce aggregated and average price gain; storage throughput; families insured; scholarships awarded; and **cooperative turnover and surplus.**
- **Review cadence:** quarterly PMC review, annual independent/IFFCO review, and a live digital dashboard maintained by the Society.

---

## 14. Governance, transparency and safeguards

- **Ring-fenced, independently audited** project account; public display of works and beneficiary lists.
- **Conflict-of-interest protocol:** office-bearers holding political or municipal posts **recuse themselves** from procurement and beneficiary-selection decisions; procurement follows cooperative rules and competitive quotations.
- **Transparent, needs-based beneficiary selection** prioritising small and marginal farmers.
- **Precondition:** the Society's accounts and audit must be in good standing before disbursement.

## 15. Risk analysis and mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Rainfall failure delaying water works | Medium | Sequence works to monsoon; prioritise recharge + micro-irrigation |
| Cooperative governance/audit gaps | Medium | Pre-sanction audit; PMU oversight; ring-fenced account |
| Under-utilised machinery/drone | Medium | Demand-linked rollout; service pricing; trained local operators |
| Slow market-linkage uptake | Medium | Anchor on own aggregation + IFFCO Bazar; buyer MoUs |
| Convergence funds not materialising | Medium | Grant designed to stand alone; convergence is upside, not dependency |
| Conflict-of-interest perception | Low–Medium | Formal recusal protocol; transparent procurement |
