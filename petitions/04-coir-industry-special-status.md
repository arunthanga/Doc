**Subject:** Request for special status and pollution-category parity for coir de-fibring and coir-pith processing units in Chittur, so that the coir industry can develop within Kerala instead of migrating across the border to Pollachi (Tamil Nadu).

---

## 1. Respected Sir,

Chittur adjoins the Pollachi belt of Tamil Nadu and has abundant coconut and coir-husk availability. Yet the primary coir-processing industry has migrated almost entirely to Pollachi, chiefly because of a **difference in pollution-control classification** between the two States. We pray for a level playing field.

## 2. The factual position

1. **The classification gap.** The primary coir processes — **coconut-husk retting, de-fibring, and coir-pith processing** — are treated far more stringently by the Kerala State Pollution Control Board (historically in the **Red / Orange** category) than in Tamil Nadu. In **October 2023 the Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board withdrew its earlier "Orange" reclassification and retained coir husk-retting, de-fibring and pith-processing in the "White" (practically non-polluting) category**.

2. **The consequence.** Because of this gap, the **polluting / primary processes (de-fibring, pith buffering) are carried out in Pollachi**, while only value-addition (mats, yarn, mattings, geotextiles) is done in Kerala. Coir entrepreneurs — including many from Kerala — have set up primary units in Pollachi, taking away investment, jobs and the value of our own coconut husk.

3. **CPCB norms actually permit a lighter category.** The **CPCB Directions dated 07-03-2016** (No. B-29012/ESS(CPA)/2015-16), issued under **Section 18(1)(b) of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974**, harmonised the classification on a **Pollution Index (PI)** basis — **Red (PI ≥ 60), Orange (41–59), Green (21–40), White (≤ 20)** — and this has been carried forward by the **CPCB Directions dated 12-02-2025**. Under this methodology and the current classification, coir is placed at a **much lighter category than Red**:
   - Coir **fibre / pith processing generating effluent — Green**;
   - Coir fibre / pith processing and manufacture of coir products by **dry process — White**;
   - Only coir **bleaching / dyeing / printing — Orange**.

   For comparison, the **TNPCB Board Proceedings dated 10-11-2021** briefly moved coir from White to Orange, but this was **withdrawn in 2023**, retaining coconut-husk retting, de-fibring and pith-processing in the **White** category in Tamil Nadu. In other words, a blanket Red/Orange treatment of ordinary de-fibring and dry pith processing in Kerala is **stricter than the CPCB norm requires**, and the Kerala State Pollution Control Board can align (under the same 2016/2025 Directions) without diluting genuine environmental safeguards.

## 3. Our prayer

We humbly request the Hon'ble Minister, in coordination with the Coir portfolio and the Kerala State Pollution Control Board, to:

1. **Re-align the pollution-category of coir de-fibring and dry coir-pith processing** in Kerala to **Green / White**, consistent with the CPCB 2016 methodology and the 2023/2025 classification, so that such units are viable in Chittur while dyeing/bleaching alone remains regulated.

2. Grant **special coir-cluster / coir industrial-zone status for Chittur**, with a Special Purpose Vehicle and a **Common Facility Centre** (mechanised de-fibring, pith washing / block-making with **Zero-Liquid-Discharge and effluent / salinity management**), so that environmental concerns are addressed collectively and cost-effectively.

3. Provide **fiscal incentives** — capital subsidy, interest subvention, and **power-tariff parity with Tamil Nadu** — to attract coir units to Chittur.

4. Ensure a **level playing field with Pollachi** so that the value addition to Kerala's own coconut husk is retained within Kerala, generating local employment.
