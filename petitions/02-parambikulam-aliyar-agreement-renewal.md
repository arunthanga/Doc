## 1. Respected Sir,

The single most important lifeline for the farmers of the Chittur rain-shadow belt is the water of the **Chitturpuzha**, a tributary of the Bharathapuzha, fed under the inter-State **Parambikulam–Aliyar Project (PAP)** agreement with Tamil Nadu. We pray for the State's urgent intervention to renew this long-overdue agreement and to secure the water that is rightfully ours.

## 2. Why Chittur has no alternative

Chittur is the **eastern-most** agricultural tract of Kerala. Almost every river rising in the Western Ghats flows **westward**; **no west-flowing river can reach** the eastern panchayats of Eruthempathy, Vadakarapathy and Kozhinjampara. Combined with the lowest rainfall in the State (about 1,000 mm against Kerala's ~3,000 mm average), this makes the assured release under the PAP agreement — chiefly through the **Manakadavu weir** into the Chitturpuzha and thence the **Moolathara barrage / Right Bank Canal** — the **only** dependable surface-water source for this region. The waters of the Chittur valley were historically developed to serve the Chittur tract of the erstwhile **Cochin (Kochi) State**; the command that this water must serve has since expanded well beyond that original region.

## 3. The agreement and its shortfall — the facts

1. **The agreement.** The PAP agreement was signed between Kerala and Tamil Nadu on **29 May 1970**, with retrospective effect from **9 November 1958**.

2. **Renewal is decades overdue.** The agreement contains a clause for **review every 30 years**, i.e. it was **due for review in 1988**. Despite many rounds of official- and political-level talks, it **has still not been renewed** — a delay now of nearly four decades.

3. **Kerala's assured share is not being received.** Under the agreement, Kerala is entitled to about **7,250 million cubic feet (≈ 7.25 TMC)** of water per water year (1 July – 10 June) at the **Manakadavu weir** for the Chitturpuzha, in addition to an entitlement of about **2.5 TMC from the Parambikulam group of reservoirs for the exclusive use of the Chitturpuzha valley**. In practice, actual releases fall far short:
   - In the **2023–24 water year, Kerala received only about 3,573 mcft** (as reported to the Assembly up to January 2024) — roughly **half** the assured 7,250 mcft.
   - The Kerala Assembly's **1994 ad-hoc committee** recorded that Kerala had received only about **15.9 TMC against a due share of 41.75 TMC**.

4. **Structural cause.** Following the construction of the chain of PAP dams and diversions, the natural flow that once reached the Chitturpuzha valley has been substantially curtailed; assured flows at Manakadavu and Sholayar are not being honoured in low-yield years, and Tamil Nadu has proposed further diversions without Kerala's concurrence.

## 4. Our prayer

We humbly request the Hon'ble Minister, and through him the Hon'ble Chief Minister (who holds the Inter-State River Waters portfolio), to:

1. **Accord top priority to the renewal / review of the PAP agreement**, which has been overdue since 1988, and pursue it at the Chief-Minister level with Tamil Nadu.

2. **Insist on full realisation of the assured 7,250 mcft (7.25 TMC) at the Manakadavu weir**, with **telemetry / measuring stations and a transparent monitoring mechanism** to guarantee the flow, especially in the critical summer months and in low-yield years.

3. Press Tamil Nadu to **release water from the Parambikulam reservoir to Aliyar and onward to Manakadavu**, and to honour the **2.5 TMC exclusive Chitturpuzha-valley entitlement**.

4. **Reconstitute and reconvene the joint (secretary-level) Kerala–Tamil Nadu committee**; and, should Tamil Nadu remain unresponsive, examine recourse to a **statutory tribunal / Union Government (Ministry of Jal Shakti) mediation** under the inter-State river-water framework.

5. **Complete and extend the Moolathara Right Bank Canal micro-irrigation project** so that the assured water actually reaches the tail-end fields of Eruthempathy, Vadakarapathy and Kozhinjampara.
