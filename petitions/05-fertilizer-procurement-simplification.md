**Subject:** Request to simplify the procurement of urea and other subsidised fertilizers for genuine farmers — especially the elderly and tenant cultivators — while strengthening safeguards against diversion to the wrong persons.

---

## 1. Respected Sir,

Primary Agricultural Co-operative Societies (PACS) and co-operative outlets are the main channel through which our farmers buy fertilizer. The present procedure, though well-intentioned, has become **burdensome for genuine farmers** while still leaving gaps that allow diversion. We pray for a simpler, safer system.

## 2. The factual position

1. **The current procedure — the exact position.** Fertilizer sale and movement are governed by the **Fertilizer (Control) Order, 1985** made under the **Essential Commodities Act, 1955**. The Union Department of Fertilizers introduced **Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) in Fertilizers from October 2016** (pilot), launched the **Integrated Fertilizer Management System (iFMS) in June 2016**, and **completed the pan-India rollout on 1 March 2018**. Under the **Aadhaar-enabled Fertilizers Distribution System (AeFDS)**, all subsidised fertilizers — **urea, DAP, MOP, super-phosphate, etc.** — can be sold only through **Aadhaar-linked Point-of-Sale (PoS) machines with biometric authentication**, the buyer being identified via **Aadhaar / Kisan Credit Card / Voter ID**. Notably, the Department's own guidelines provide that the crop/area recommendation is **not binding** and sale is on a **"no-denial mode"** — a principle that should protect genuine farmers from being turned away for want of documents.

2. **The difficulties on the ground.** In Palakkad, farmers report:
   - **Repeated demands for documents** — Aadhaar, and in practice **land-tax receipts / land records** — at every purchase;
   - **Fingerprint-authentication failures**, especially for **elderly farmers** and manual labourers;
   - **Exclusion of tenant / lease cultivators**, who till land they do not own and cannot readily produce land documents;
   - **Network / technical glitches** at PoS terminals, and problems with small-quantity purchases.

3. **The genuine concern.** At the same time, safeguards are needed so that subsidised fertilizer is **not diverted** to non-agricultural / industrial use, bulk hoarders, or across the State border to the wrong persons.

## 3. Our prayer

We humbly request the Hon'ble Minister for Co-operation (through whom the PACS network functions), in coordination with Agriculture and Civil Supplies, to:

1. **Introduce a one-time verified "Farmer ID / registration"** — issued through the PACS and linked once to the farmer's **Aadhaar, land / registered-lease record and Soil Health Card** — so that documents such as land-tax receipts **need not be produced at every purchase**; the PoS simply reads the pre-verified entitlement.

2. **Permit biometric alternatives** (OTP / face authentication) and allow an **authorised family member or registered nominee** to purchase on behalf of elderly, ill or differently-abled farmers.

3. **Recognise registered tenant / lease cultivators** (through Padasekhara Samithis / lease registers / cultivation certificates from the Krishi Bhavan) so that landless-but-genuine farmers are not excluded.

4. **Do not insist on the land-tax receipt at each sale** once the one-time registration is complete.

5. **Strengthen anti-diversion safeguards** — enforce **crop-wise / area-wise entitlement ceilings** in the PoS / iFMS, flag abnormal or bulk purchases, and audit — so that fertilizer reaches only genuine cultivators and is blocked from wrong persons and cross-border diversion.

6. **Ensure adequate buffer stock and upgraded PoS connectivity** at PACS in Eruthempathy, Vadakarapathy and Kozhinjampara during the peak sowing season.
