# Chittur Delegation to Thiruvananthapuram — 15 July 2026

**Delegation:** Representatives of the **Chittur Agriculture Marketing Society, Kozhinjampara**, Chittur Taluk, Palakkad District, Kerala.

**Accompanied by:** **Adv. Sumesh Achuthan, MLA** — Chittur (Constituency No. 58), Kerala Legislative Assembly.

**Purpose:** To submit petitions and request the concerned Ministers to expedite action on five issues affecting the Chittur Assembly constituency, and in particular the rain-shadow panchayats of **Eruthempathy, Vadakarapathy and Kozhinjampara**.

## Note on facts and figures

These drafts were prepared using publicly reported data (Central Ground Water Board, Kerala Ground Water Department, Kerala Irrigation Department / KIIDC, Milma/MRCMPU, CPCB/TNPCB, Department of Fertilizers). A few figures in the original brief were corrected against the record:

- **Rainfall** is measured in **millimetres (mm)**, not centimetres. Chittur's rain-shadow belt gets roughly **≤1,000 mm** a year against Kerala's average of about **3,000 mm** — i.e. about one-third.
- The bottling-plant water controversy referred to is the **Plachimada (Coca-Cola) case in Perumatty panchayat**, Chittur — used here as background to the restrictive groundwater regime.
- **PAP water figures** are stated using documented entitlements (**7.25 TMC / 7,250 mcft** at Manakadavu weir) rather than the "90 / 12.5 TMC" figures in the brief.
